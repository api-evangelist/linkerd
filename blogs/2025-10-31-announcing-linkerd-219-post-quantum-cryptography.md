<p>Today we&rsquo;re happy to announce Linkerd 2.19! This release introduces a
significant state-of-the-art security improvement for Linkerd: a modernized TLS
stack that uses post-quantum key exchange algorithms by default.</p>
<p>Linkerd has now seen almost a decade of continuous improvement and evolution.
Our goal is to build a service mesh that our users can rely on for 100 years. To
do this, we
<a href="https://www.grammarly.com/blog/engineering/the-great-linkerd-mystery/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">partner with users like Grammarly to ensure that Linkerd can accelerate the full scale and scope of modern software environments</a>—and
then we feed those lessons directly back into the product. Linkerd 2.19 release
is the third major version since the
<a href="https://buoyant.io/blog/linkerd-forever" rel="noopener" target="_blank">announcement of Buoyant&rsquo;s profitability and Linkerd project sustainability a year ago</a>,
and continues our laser focus on operational simplicity—delivering the
notoriously complex service mesh feature set in a way that is manageable,
scalable, and performant.</p>
